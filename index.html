What is Polymerase Chain Reaction?

Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) is a laboratory technique used to rapidly amplify specific segments of DNA. It enables scientists to study and detect even small amounts of genetic material.

Processes of Polymerase Chain Reaction

PCR amplifies DNA through three main steps:

  • Denaturation: Separating DNA strands
  • Annealing: Primers bind to DNA
  • Extension: DNA polymerase synthesizes new strands
